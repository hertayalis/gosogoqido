    Camilla Sparv (born June 3, 1943) is a Swedish actress who has appeared in many films. In 1965, she had a short marriage to American film producer Robert Evans, which ended in divorce. Sparv was born on June 3, 1943, in the Swedish capital of Stockholm. When she appeared in Dead Heat on a Merry-Go-Round, with James Coburn, she was nominated for a Golden Globe Award for Most Promising Newcomer (Female), which she received in 1967. (1966). Among her other film and television credits are Murderers’ Row (1966), The Trouble with Angels (1966), Assignment K (1968), Mackenna’s Gold (1969), Downhill Racer (1969), The Greek Tycoon (1978), The Greek Tycoon (1980), and Caboblanco (1980), as well as the television shows The Rockford Files, The Love Boat, and Hawaii Five-O. The premiere episode of the ABC crime thriller The Feather and Father Gang, “Never Con a Killer,” was filmed in 1977, and she starred in it. Sparv is now retired, and she has two children from her second marriage. She has been married to her third husband, Fred Kolber, since June 1994, and they have three children together.
