    Adam Carolla | Lifestyle and Vacations

    Lifestyle

    Multi-millionaire radio personality Adam Carolla lives a luxurious lifestyle with a net worth of $20 million.

    Adam was born in Los Angeles, California, on May 27, 1964.

    With his mother Kris, father Jim, and older sister Lauren, he experienced childhood in Wisconsin, Pennsylvania, and North Hollywood.

    Adam claims that his mother struggled to provide for her children after his parents divorced.

    He attended Colfax Elementary School and Walter Reed Junior High before graduating from North Hollywood High School in 2005 after paying off a long-standing school library charge.

    Adam played Pop Warner football for seven years as a junior and was named to the Central Valley League’s First Team Offensive Line in high school.

    At the age of 18, he chose to live independently and entered Los Angeles Valley College, but he quit after being placed on academic probation to focus on work.

    He worked as a carpenter, driving coach, carpet cleaner, and boxing coach.

    In the early 1990s, Carolla began his comic practice with The Groundlings and ACME Comedy Theatre.

    He volunteered as a boxing trainer in 1994 to train Jimmy Kimmel for a fight sponsored by KROQ-morning FM’s radio show “Kevin and Bean.”

    As a result, he turned this chance into a long-term friendship and business connection with Kimmel.

    Adam enjoys collecting cars and racing them. In fact, he has won two Pro/Celebrity races, one as a celebrity in 2012 and the other as a professional in 2013.

    In 2002, Adam wedded Lynette Paradise, and on June 7, 2006, the couple delivered twins Santino and Natalia.

    However, the couple announced their divorce in May 2021, after 19 years of marriage.

    Adam Carolla | Career

    Adam was offered a job co-hosting the “Loveline” radio program when he was signed to the William Morris Agency in 1995.

    “Loveline” became nationally televised, and Carolla remained with the program until November 2005.

    After that, he began anchoring “The Adam Carolla Show” on the Infinity Broadcasting network in January 2006.

    After KLSX became a top 40 station in February 2009, the show was canceled.

    Adam launched “The Adam Carolla Podcast” on February 23, 2009, which airs daily on his website.

    It immediately rose to the top of iTunes, where “The Adam Carolla Podcast” was crowned the Best Audio Podcast of 2009.

    Carolla and Dr. Drew aired “Loveline” on MTV from 1996 to 2000, a television adaptation of the radio program.

    From 1999 to 2003, Adam co-hosted his first exclusive television series, “The Man Show,” on Comedy Central alongside colleague and buddy Jimmy Kimmel.
